Measure Theory

Introduction: a non-measurable set

Interval open at a and close a b
(0) Defination: $\lambda(\left(a,b\right]) = b-a\ $ (a mesure of the interval should be equal to the length of the interval)

(1) $\lambda: \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}_+ \cup +\infty$, which extends the idea of length to all subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ ($\mathcal{P}$ is all subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ and $\lambda$ is defined for all the subset of $\mathbb{R}$)

(2) Translation invariance: $ A\subset \mathbb{R}, A + x = \left\{x + y, y \in A\right\},\quad \forall A\subset \mathbb{R}, x \in \mathbb{R}: \lambda(A + x) = \lambda(A)$

(3) Countable additivity(sigma additive): $A = \cup_{j}A_j,\quad $ if $ A_j\cap A_k = \emptyset,\quad \lambda(A) = \sum_{j} = \lambda(A_j)$

$x \sim y,\ x,y\in \mathbb{R}$ if $x-y \in \mathbb{Q}$

$[x] = \left\{ y \in \mathbb{R}, y-x \in \mathbb{Q} \right\}$

$\Lambda = \mathbb{R}|_{\sim} $
